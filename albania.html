Discover the Heart of the Balkans

Unveil the beauty, history, and culture of Albania.

History of Albania

Albania has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was once the home of the Illyrians, and later became part of the Roman Empire. In the medieval period, it was known for its powerful kingdoms and its strategic position along trade routes. Albania declared independence from the Ottoman Empire in 1912 and has since developed its unique cultural identity.

Culture of Albania

Albania’s culture is a blend of influences from the Illyrians, Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans. The country is known for its traditional music, dance, and crafts. Folk art plays a significant role in Albanian society, and the people are renowned for their hospitality. Traditional dishes like "byrek" (savory pastry) and "tavë kosi" (lamb with yogurt) are central to Albanian cuisine.

Geography of Albania

Albania is located in Southeastern Europe on the western side of the Balkan Peninsula. It has a stunning coastline along the Adriatic and Ionian Seas. The country is mountainous, with many national parks and scenic spots. Notable geographical features include the Albanian Riviera, the Llogara Pass, and Lake Shkodra, the largest lake in the Balkans.

Flag Day

Coinciding with Independence Day, Flag Day celebrates the raising of the Albanian flag in Vlora in 1912. It is a day of national pride and is celebrated with various cultural events and displays of the national flag.
